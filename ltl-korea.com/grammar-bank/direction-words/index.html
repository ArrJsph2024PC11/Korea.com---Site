    HOW TO ASK FOR DIRECTIONS

    How to go to school? Where is the cinema?

    Today we’re teaching you how to ask and give directions in Korean!

    Make sure to also check our lesson on direction particles -로 and -으로.

    ASKING FOR DIRECTIONS

    To ask for a direction in Korean, you first state the destination you want, and use a question form that generally follows the structure below: 

    PATTERN

    어느 (which) + 쪽으로 (direction) / 방향으로 (direction) / 길로 (street) + 가면될까요 (should I take)?

    There are also ways to ask for a direction in less specific way, such as:

    시청은 어디쯔음 있습니까?

    About where is the City Hall?

    시청에는 어떻게 가면 됩니까?

    How do I get to the City Hall?

    Q&A EXAMPLES

    Here are a few more sentence examples in the form of questions and answers, to help you see how it works in a dialogue between two persons.

    시청에는 어떻게 가면 될까요?

    How do I get to the City Hall?

    이 길 쭈욱 따라 가시면 씨네마가 있는데 거기서 왼쪽으로 올라가시면 되요.

    If you go down this street, you will see a cinema; there, you take left and go up. 

    공원은 어디쯔음 있나요?

    About where is the park? 

    저 다리 밑을 지나가셔서 오른쪽으로 가세요. 공원은 대학가와 금융가 사이에 있어요.

    Go under that bridge and turn right. The park is located between the university campus and the financial district.

    FAQs

    How do you say Left in Korean?

    Left in Korean is 왼쪽 (oenjjok).

    Examples:

    Turn left: 왼쪽으로 돌으세요.

    (좌회전 하세요 – typically used for driving directions)

    It is on your left: 왼쪽에 있어요.

    How do you say Right in Korean?

    Right in Korean is 오른쪽 (oreunjjok).

    Examples:

    Turn right: 오른쪽으로 돌으세요.

    (우회전 하세요 – typically used for driving directions)

    It is on your right: 오른쪽에 있어요.

    How do you say Next to in Korean?

    Next to in Korean is 옆에 (yeope) or 옆쪽에 (yeopjjoge).

    Examples:

    The cinema is next to the market.

    씨네마는 마트 옆에 있어요.

    씨네마는 마트 옆쪽에 있어요.

    How do you say Between in Korean?

    Between in Korean is 사이에 (sa i e).

    Example:

    공원은 대학과 금융가 사이에 있어요.

    The park is between university campus and the financial district.
